# Polar Rover
Software navigation and hardware specifications for LDEO rover. This project is supported by the OTIC Paros fund.

# Background
Polar environments are some of the most logistically challenging, dangerous, and expensive research sites on Earth. For every $1 the National Science Foundation (NSF) spends on Antarctic Science, they spend $4 on logistics. Work in polar regions is also physically demanding, presenting barriers to entry for folks who may be less able to lift and operate heavy equipment and spend long hours working in extreme-cold conditions. Hazards and barriers lead to critical data gaps, limiting accessibility and reproducibility of field measurements. To fill these knowledge gaps, improve field safety, increase accessibility, and enable new observations and measurements that require precise positioning and synchronization, we are building two modular autonomous rovers. These platforms will be designed to collect data that will complement conventional acquisition methods in remote field environments while enabling new measurements that require linked autonomy and synchronization.

# Measurement Goals
1. Build two functioning rover platforms.
2. Develop dual-band GNSS waypoint navigation and surface hazard assessment via onboard cameras and lidar.
3. Develop linked navigation capabilities over VHF radio communication.
